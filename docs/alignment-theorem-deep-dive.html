Purpose

Design a robust deflationary economy on Tau Net that uses boolean-function agents, formal invariants, MEV/oracle safety, upgrade wrappers, and demand sinks. Content condenses a 100-iteration research loop with Atom of Thoughts reasoning.

Pillars

  • Deflationary kernel agents with structural burn sinks and bounded emissions.
  • MEV and oracle hardening through commit-reveal, banding, cooldowns, and replayable traces.
  • Demand formation via POL, ve locks, staking sinks, auctions, and bonding curves.
  • Governance wrappers that guard pointwise revisions with invariants.
  • Verification-first design: BF monitors, frontier-aware factoring, and deterministic trace capture.

Core Math

  • Gate-first additive bound: shared gate literal first yields ROBDD size ≤ 1 + Σ|Ci|.
  • Frontier-width gating: select gates early to reduce max frontier during normalization.
  • Quantification cost model: T ≈ poly · 2^support · κ(block) → minimize offsets and cluster signals.
  • Tight bounds: parity demands 2^n nodes; ripple adders reach O(n) when interleaved.

Economic Mechanisms

  • Base-fee burns echo EIP-1559: burns act as reward for ethical throughput.
  • Buyback-and-burn vs buyback-and-make toggles encoded as policy booleans.
  • Protocol-owned liquidity (POL) reduces mercenary LP risk and is guarded by monitors.
  • Strict circuit breakers ensure seigniorage-style components cannot run uncollateralized.

MEV / Oracle Templates

  • Commit-reveal phases `commit_ok`, `reveal_ok` gate sensitive state transitions.
  • Batch auctions ensure execution only at settlement boundaries `batch_window_open`.
  • TWA-based cooldown timers enforce paced execution to dampen frontrunning.
  • Freshness and band checks (`fresh`, `in_band`) guard every oracle feed.
  • MEV tax routing ensures captured MEV flows into sinks or treasury burn.

Demand Sinks

  • ve locks with boolean monitor `lock_active` for voting power.
  • Staking sinks map `staked -> non_circulating`; slash events burn.
  • Auctions and bonding curves rely on `demand_event` inputs with strict overspend guards.

Invariant Catalog

  • Mutual exclusion: `mutex2(buy,sell)` prevents conflicting actions.
  • No-overspend: `spend_event -> budget_ok` for treasury safety.
  • Fresh execution gating: `(progress' | (state' | fresh))` eliminates ghost actions.
  • Monotone burn: `burn' | profit` ensures no reverse edges.
  • Nonce discipline and assume-guarantee composition across modules.

Agent Templates

  • Gray-coded timers for pacing and cooldowns.
  • Progress gating to avoid t = 0 artifacts and latch flips.
  • Shared enable literal `en := exec & buy' & sell'` reused across SOP cubes.

Iteration Highlights

  1. Early gating shrinks frontier pathwidth and avoids ROBDD blowups.
  2. XOR removal plus helper factoring keeps normalization linear in clauses.
  3. Group sifting + literal clustering preserve cache locality.
  4. Token-bucket pacing combined with cooldown inputs counters MEV oscillations.
  5. Median-of-medians oracles with slash-to-burn incentives resist manipulation.
  6. Buyback + POL loops convert EETF uplifts into compounding scarcity.
  7. Progress monitors and L2S counters certify bounded execution windows.
